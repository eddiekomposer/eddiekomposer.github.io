Google Design Exercise

A one-week design project as part of Google recruitment process. All work was done by me personally including research, interviews and analysis, user flows, sketches, wireframes, hi-fidelity prototypes, and usability testing.

Prompt

Your school wants to provide dedicated spaces for students to help them focus on their academic studies. Design an experience for students, professors, and members of the school community to reserve work desks / spaces in the library. Consider the experience of checking availability, reserving the space, and reporting any issues.

Assumptions

  • Where?
    Pao Yue-Kong Library of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University

  • What is the goal?
    A user-friendly experience is desired for library space booking in my school

  • Who are the stakeholders?
    Students:     Main group of the experience. There are individuals and group users
    Professors: Reserve space in a lower frequency since most of them have their own office spaces
    Other:           Alumni and staff

PROBLEM

Great minds think alike

Library is the best place to study and work: 
it gives users convenient access to academic resources, positive learning atmosphere and cozy environment.

There is an obvious traffic increase during specific time period (midterm, final etc.) and it is desperate when it is almost impossible to find a seat.

An easy way to reserve library space could liberate students from space availability worries and let them better focus on their study.

Needs

The reservation process should be easy and understandable (especially for freshman)

Clear instructions after reservation (status, notifications, guides)

Different context (make a plan in advance or walk in and reserve spontaneously, individual or group)

Humanistic care (help & consultation)

Some Facts...

Among the users, undergraduates were most concerned with the service quality of the Library as Place dimension.

Overall, our users found the Library's “comfortable and inviting location” as the most favorable aspect of the Library

“Quiet space for individual work” was the most important to all three user groups (undergrad, grad, staff)

There’s a need for more flexible timeslot for reservation and the library’s response is: To meet the needs of a variety of users, bookable sessions for the Research Carrels are variable duration. session durations range from 2-hour sessions to the longer 8-hour session, each suited to different type of users.

HUMAN CENTERED INTERVIEW

Research Objective Statement

To understand what is the highest concern in a booking experience

Knowledge Goals

  • Understand Process
    What is your overall procedure of library space reservation?
    When will you reserve? How long will it be in advance?
    How will you reserve? By using what tool?
    Have you ever failed obtaining a space? why and what’s your response?

  • Understand Motivation
    Why will you reserve a space?
    What about walk in?
    What are the things you feel unsatisfied during the process?

  • Understand How They See The World
    What makes you feel relieved?
    How do you see your life is different in a reserved space than in your home?
    How the interactions between machine, environment and other people affect you?

Analysis

Key Insights & Opportunities

  Hidden User

For group activities there will be only one person booking the space but every member is the user of this reservation experience

 Varying Needs

Before reservation availability is more important while after reservation time and location is more important

 Viscosity

Everyone has his/her own usual spot

 Distance

People prefer seats that not close to other people

Experience of all the people envolved should be considered

The user flow needs to be adjusted accordingly

Quick reservation based on last reservation is good

Show the user the best seat, not the first seat

And what’s beyond reservation itself...

Reservation is just a start of a study / work happened in the library, which is what the library wants to better serve. 
How can we show empathy and humanistic care to our users to support their study/work and improve the whole experience?

Wireframe

User Testing

Takeaways

Multiuser Support
Even for individual users looking for quiet spaces, some may want to go to the lib with their peers and stay close to each other. When checking availability, they also want to see which space other people is booking

Offline Experience
The experience is not limited as screen-based. Some people may schedule their reservation in advance but some may walk in and want to reserve spontaneously

Usability Improvement
Adding minor features and debugging. Adjusted buttons like cancel, extend, back and changed layout to make it more intuitive and understandable

HI-FIDELITY PROTOTYPE

Visual Language
Use colors and fonts which match the university’s personality and also considering about screen readability

Keep it simple and straightforward
show the most important info on the main screen and remove unnecessary elements

New Reservation

The best seat (good availability, not close to the crowd) will show first

Red block: unavailable timeslot
yellow block: timeslot someone else is editing

Editing Reservation

Tap and drag on the blank area to create a reservation

Swipe up to confirm

Homepage

Homepage will change to show the most important information during different situations

Be friendly and empathetic to the users

Anthropomorphic

People always want to talk with real person. Even when there is no available librarian yet, the interface creates a vibe and makes users believe their feedback is received and reviewed carefully
