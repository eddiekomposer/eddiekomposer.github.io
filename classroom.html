Classroom

Classroom is an online teaching tool designed to enhance teachers’ engagement with students. It creates a course-centric context for teachers to better schedule their daily work and communicate with students.

CLASSROOM

where teachers meet students freely.

Classroom provides a consistent workflow for teachers so they don’t get lost in the challenging remote environment

START

In Classroom teachers can easily manage all the stuff related to their work systematically at one place. They can follow a daily plan to better manage their time schedule and avoid missing any important tasks or events

RESPONSIVE TEACHING PLAN

Classroom allows teachers to prepare their online class in a smart way - They can prepare class sections, resources or rooms in advance which will be the clue to guide their teaching later during online meetings

CLEAR THEME

Resources, recordings or files are sorted in a timeline order automatically. When talk, work and share with students, teachers will always keep a clear mind about what topic they are talking about, which improves communication effectiveness

The platform is designed for better communication and collaboration by teachers and students

AFFORDABLE FOR EVERYONE

Classroom is a webpage app entered via your browser, anytime and anywhere;
It’s convenient to continue your work on public or rented devices.
Classroom also offers innovative techniques like face-tracking avatars to promise user experience on low-quality network

COMPATIBILITY

Users can view, edit and move files before downloading;
Files can be used in diverse ways: It can be shared during meeting, sent in messages or mails, edited by multiusers simultaneously and more... 

VIVID INTERACTIONS

Emotions are very important for better engagement. In Classroom, users can express their emotions in subtle ways such as dynamic video windows, virtual body languages and instant learning feedback.

Classroom provides humanistic cares everywhere in order to create a good atmosphere which is the key to a successful class

KEEP YOU ENERGETIC!

Design details are used to create an energetic and positive working environment for users, incluidng greetings, visualized data analysis and personalized suggestions

EMOTIONAL CONNECTION

Classroom uses familiar concepts to lower users’ study cost and decrease uneasy feelings when they move their activities online.
For example, each course has a ‘classroom’ where users can leave messages on the ‘whiteboard’, submit assignments via the ‘podium desktop’ or have private discussions by entering another person’s seat.

DESIGN PROCESS

BACKGROUND

This is a UX project for software company SAP. They were developing design concepts for a tool that helps teachers to engage with students in a distant learning environment.
I was given 5 hours to do a whiteboard practise to give a preliminary idea for that. Later I developed my idea further and realized the concept with this product. 

Problem

An effective and easy way to engage with students is missing after schools shut down and teachers have to move to a distant learning environment.

Goal Statement

Design something to help teachers better engage with students in a distant learning environment.

Industry Background

While countries are at different points in their COVID-19 infection rates, worldwide there are currently more than 1.2 billion children in 186 countries affected by school closures due to the pandemic.

Even before COVID-19, there was already high growth and adoption in education technology, with global edtech investments reaching US$18.66 billion in 2019 and the overall market for online education projected to reach $350 Billion by 2025. Whether it is language apps, virtual tutoring, video conferencing tools, or online learning software, there has been a significant surge in usage since COVID-19.

Trends

While some believe that the unplanned and rapid move to online learning with no training, insufficient bandwidth, and little preparation will result in a poor user experience that is unconducive to sustained growth, others believe that a new hybrid model of education will emerge, with significant benefits.

Premise

To have a clear direction of my design, I made the necessary assumptions as following:

Solution Type

Consider about the speciality of education industry, this should not be a commercial project which puts profit on the highest priority. Instead, I think it would not be a successful solution unless it considers needs from all groups of educators and students, such as low socioeconomics and minorities. So I imagine the solution being a web app. It’s easily accessible from all platforms, easy to learn, and friendly for passable devices and network.

Application Scale

To avoid bloatware, I consider the mainstream education from K-12 to higher education first. Other minor education forms is not included, such as dancing, music or cooking.

Definition of Engagement

In this context, the definition of engagement will be the interactions that happen between teacher and student, before class, during class and after class. There are technical engagement (activities that teachers adopt to assess educational effectiveness, manage progress and connect with students) as well as humanistic engagement (activities that teachers adopt to build emotional connection with students).

PRODUCT

Iteration Process

I tested the early prototypes with target users and iterated my design in a systematic way:

1st round: Rough Sketches

To test the overall userflow

2nd round: Low-fidelity Mock-ups

To test the usability based on A/B testing and Nielsen’s usability principles

3rd round: High-fidelity Prototypes

To test the design system consistency, visual effects and functional layouts

Findings & Improvements

1. Before I started from creating an experience which took advantage of large screens, so the layouts are multitask-based. During the test some teachers mentioned that they have plenty of time but unclear tasks. So I changed the interface layouts with more single purpose screens and step by step instructions to better guide their work;

2. Most teachers still do their work with desktop devices. So I improved the interface considering more for desktop operations. Some examples include snap and collapsed cards, more compact element size and individual windows etc;

3. I simplified the userflow by deleting the functions in ‘start’ screen and adding links there so users can jump to corresponding screens which does not cause confusion.
